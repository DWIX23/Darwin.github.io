BS Biology

The BS Biology program is a degree program that provides students with a comprehensive understanding of living organisms and their processes. The program covers a range of topics such as genetics, ecology, microbiology, and evolution. Graduates of the program can pursue careers in research, academia, healthcare, and other related fields.

Animal Biology
publishes high quality papers and focuses on integration of the various disciplines within the broad field of zoology. These disciplines include behaviour, developmental biology, ecology, endocrinology, evolutionary biology, genomics, morphology, neurobiology, physiology, systematics and theoretical biology. Purely descriptive papers will not be considered for publication.

Microbiology
microbiology, study of microorganisms, or microbes, a diverse group of generally minute simple life-forms that include bacteria, archaea, algae, fungi, protozoa, and viruses. The field is concerned with the structure, function, and classification of such organisms and with ways of both exploiting and controlling their activities.

scientific discipline that studies mental states and processes and behaviour in humans and other animals. The discipline of psychology is broadly divisible into two parts: a large profession of practitioners and a smaller but growing science of mind, brain, and social behaviour. The two have distinctive goals, training, and practices, but some psychologists integrate the two.

Plant Biology
Plant biology is the study of plant life and growth. Another name for this branch of biology is botany. It encompasses a wide range of scientific fields concerned with plant research. Structure, growth, reproduction, metabolism, development, illnesses, and chemical qualities, as well as evolutionary links between groupings, are among them. Botany was founded by tribal efforts to identify edible, medicinal, and dangerous plants, making it one of the earliest sciences.
